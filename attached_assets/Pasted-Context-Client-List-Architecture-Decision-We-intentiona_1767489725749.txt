Context: Client List Architecture Decision

We intentionally changed the client list to display locations (sites) instead of only parent company names, and this was a deliberate architectural decision, not a UI preference.

Reasoning:

Operational scope
In the HVAC/service domain, all operational entities are location-scoped:

Jobs

Equipment

Maintenance schedules

Calendar assignments

Service history

Often invoices

Technicians and dispatch do not act on a company in the abstract — they act on a specific site with an address, equipment, and history.

Data model alignment
The backend already treats locations as first-class entities:

Jobs reference a location/client ID

Equipment and PM records are location-bound

Calendar entries are location-bound

Showing only companies in the primary list creates a mismatch between UI abstraction and actual data relationships, forcing additional selection steps everywhere downstream.

Multi-location companies
Many customers have multiple locations (chains, franchises, property managers). Each location may have:

Different contacts

Different billing rules

Different PM intervals

Different equipment

Listing locations directly avoids ambiguity and removes the need for secondary pickers or expand-first flows.

QuickBooks compatibility
QuickBooks Online maps cleanly to this model:

Company → customer

Location → sub-customer / job site

Treating locations as the primary operational unit simplifies invoice sync, reporting, and future accounting logic.

UX simplification
A company-only list looks cleaner initially but introduces hidden complexity:

Extra clicks to select a location

Ambiguous actions (“Which site?”)

Modal or dropdown selectors everywhere

Showing locations directly reduces friction and better reflects how the system is actually used.

Important clarification:

The company entity still exists as a parent.

This is not flattening or removing hierarchy.

This is a presentation-layer decision to surface the true operational unit.

Future flexibility:
If needed, the UI can support:

Group-by-company views

Expandable company rows

Company-level summaries

But the default list should remain location-based to align with operations, data, and accounting.

Please treat this as an intentional architectural choice and maintain consistency with this model in future UI and backend changes.
